# Private Developer Trial Checklist

Use this checklist for a small private trial with developers who already build with AI coding tools.

## Goal

Find out whether a Build Contract helps developers get a better first pass, fewer drift loops, and clearer acceptance checks.

## Participant Fit

- Uses at least one AI builder in normal work.
- Can bring a small, non-sensitive build idea.
- Can spend 30-45 minutes trying the contract flow.
- Agrees not to share confidential company data.

## Trial Flow

- Pick one scoped build idea.
- Write or adapt a Build Contract for that idea.
- Give the contract to the builder before implementation.
- Ask the builder for a pass report after each attempt.
- Compare the builder report against the contract.
- Note whether the next step is `done`, `continue`, or `ask_human`.

## What To Record

- Builder/tool used.
- Surface type, such as landing page, plugin, workflow, or API.
- Whether the contract changed the first build quality.
- Number of repair loops before the result felt acceptable.
- Which contract fields helped most.
- Which fields felt unclear or unnecessary.
- Whether the participant would use this again.

## Do Not Collect

- Secrets, customer data, or internal credentials.
- Real company strategy, unreleased product details, or proprietary datasets.
- Full transcripts unless the participant has already scrubbed them.
- Any material that would make this repo depend on non-public examples.

## Success Signals

- Participant says the contract made the builder easier to steer.
- Fewer broad rewrites or off-scope additions.
- Pass reports make review faster.
- Participants can explain what "done" means earlier in the build.

## Stop Signals

- The trial turns into general AI-builder support.
- Participants expect the kit to judge quality automatically.
- Useful feedback requires sensitive examples.
- The checklist creates more process than clarity.
